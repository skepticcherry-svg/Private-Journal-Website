Cognitive Restructuring

CBT

Challenge negative thought patterns and develop more balanced perspectives.

"Write down a negative thought you had today. What evidence supports it? What evidence challenges it?"

Behavioral Activation

CBT

Identify positive actions that can boost your mood and energy levels.

"List one small, positive action you can take today to boost your mood. How will you make time for it?"

Thought Record

CBT

Track situations, emotions, and thoughts to identify patterns.

"What situation triggered your feeling? What thought did you have? How could you reframe it more positively?"

Gratitude Reframe

CBT

Shift focus to positive aspects during challenging moments.

"Write down three things you're grateful for, especially in moments of stress. How do they change your perspective?"

Mindful Awareness

CBT

Practice present-moment awareness to reduce anxiety and overthinking.

"Take a moment to notice: What are 3 things you can see, 2 things you can hear, and 1 thing you can feel right now?"

Problem Solving

CBT

Break down challenges into manageable steps and solutions.

"Describe a current challenge. What are 3 possible solutions? What's one small step you can take today?"

How CBT Prompts Help

Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT) techniques help you identify negative thought patterns, challenge unhelpful beliefs, and develop healthier ways of thinking. These guided prompts provide structure for your reflection practice.
